Case Studies

Case Studies

Selected product initiatives showing how I approached discovery, execution, and measurable impact in sportsbook environments.

01
Frontend UX · Discovery

Player Props Discovery & Frontend Navigation

Context: Basketball player markets had strong user interest, but discovery was still dependent on deeper match-page navigation.

Problem: Valuable player props were not visible enough in the core sportsbook journey, which limited engagement and made the experience harder to scan.

What I did: I defined dedicated frontend navigation for Basketball Players markets and improved how these markets were presented in the main sportsbook structure.

Why this approach: Surfacing the market earlier in the journey was a simpler and more scalable solution than relying only on match-page entry points.

Problem

Low discoverability of player props.

Action

Dedicated frontend navigation and improved UX structure.

Result

Approx. 20% uplift in player props play rate.

Proof Artifact
02
CMS · Promo · Risk

701% Parlay Bonus — CMS Visibility & Risk Controls

Context: A high-impact promotional mechanic needed to be visible on the frontend, commercially effective, and operationally safe.

Problem: Promo journeys can increase engagement, but unclear odds presentation and weak control mechanisms can create confusion and abuse risk.

What I did: I worked on the product logic behind the 701% Parlay Bonus, including CMS-driven odds highlighting, promotional visibility, and fraud-prevention mechanics.

Why this approach: CMS-based control reduced dependency on manual changes and made the campaign easier to manage across different states and operational needs.

Problem

Complex promo flow with risk of user confusion and misuse.

Action

Defined CMS-driven frontend logic and fraud-prevention rules.

Result

Stronger promo visibility and more controlled execution.

Proof Artifact
03
White-Label · Multi-Market

Multi-Market CMS Customization for White-Label Delivery

Context: Different markets required localized homepage content, market emphasis, and promotional presentation.

Problem: A single static sportsbook setup created delivery bottlenecks and reduced flexibility for local market needs.

What I did: I helped architect CMS capabilities for white-label customization of homepage content, offers, and market-specific presentation.

Why this approach: Market-level configurability reduced engineering dependency and made it easier to support different commercial priorities across markets.

Problem

Different markets required different UX, content, and pricing emphasis.

Action

Built CMS-based white-label customization across 9 markets.

Result

Faster rollout execution and stronger local market fit.
